Oolong (乌龙茶, "black dragon tea") sits between green and black tea: the leaves are partially oxidized — anywhere from 8% to 85% — which is why no two oolongs taste alike.

A few famous ones:

- **Tieguanyin** — floral and creamy, from Fujian's Anxi county
- **Da Hong Pao** — dark, mineral "rock tea" grown on the Wuyi cliffs
- **Dong Ding** — Taiwan's roasted classic, nutty and smooth

Brew it hot (90–95°C) in a small pot with short steeps and many infusions — good leaves easily give five or more, each one a little different.
